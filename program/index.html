Program overview

Speakers

Keynote lecture

Prof. Dr. Chris de Zeeuw
Keynote lecture

    The keynote lecture will be provided by Prof. Dr. Chris de Zeeuw from Erasmus University Rotterdam and Netherlands Institute for Neuroscience.

    Title: Orchestration of Behavior by the Olivocerebellar System.


Symposia

There will be three symposia, each taking 1 hour.

Symposium 1
Relevance of the cerebellum in the clinical setting

    The first symposium will focus on the cerebellum in a clinical setting and cerebellar function underlying disease.

    Freek Hoebeek (UMC Utrecht)
     will present "The developing cerebello-thalamic connection in health and disease"

    Stacha Reumers (Radboud UMC)
     will present "The cerebellum and cognition: impairment following cerebellar stroke"

    Stefanie Keulen (Vrije Universiteit Brussel)
     will present "The linguistic cerebellum: the cerebellum as a target site for language intervention"
Symposium 2
Progress in cerebellar research techniques

    The second symposium will discuss the new developments in the techniques for cerebellar research with a focus on non-invasive methods in humans, such as EEG, MEG, sMRI, fMRI and brain stimulation.

    Jana Klaus (Utrecht University)
     will present "Measuring cerebellar signals using EEG and MEG"

    Wietske van der Zwaag (Spinoza Centre for Neuroimaging)
     will present "Structural and functional imaging of the human cerebellum in-vivo using 7T MRI"

    Roderick Maas (Radboud UMC)
    will present "Simulation of stimulation: electric field modelling in the healthy and diseased cerebellum"
Symposium 3
Fundamental cerebellar research

    The third symposium will focus on fundamental cerebellar research and the link between molecular mechanisms and behavior.

    Aleksandra Badura (Erasmus MC)
     will present on the role of cerebellum in autism: what can we learn from monogenic murine autism models?

    Wietske Zuiderbaan (Spinoza Centre for Neuroimaging)
     will present on topographic connectivity in the cerebellum for different cognitive states

    Neville Magielse (Forschungzentrum Jülich, Max Planck Institute Leipzig)
     will present on the multifaceted cerebellum: evolution, development, and large-scale meta-analysis
